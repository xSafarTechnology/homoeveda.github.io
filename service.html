Siddha

Siddha medicine is one of the oldest systems of traditional medicine practiced in southern India

Siddha medicine is one of the oldest systems of traditional medicine practiced in southern India, mainly in Tamil Nadu and Kerala. Siddha is a term widely used in Indian religions and cultures. It means "one who is realized". It refers to perfect teachers who have attained a high level of physical and mental perfection or enlightenment. In Jainism, the term is used for liberated souls. It encompasses the physical, mental, social, and spiritual well-being of the individual.

In general, Siddha treatment is very effective in treating arthritis, joint diseases, skin diseases, urinary tract infections (UTIs), kidney stones, liver problems, neurological disorders, and hemiplegia. The Siddha system also offers an effective treatment for minor ailments such as colds, coughs, and fevers.
